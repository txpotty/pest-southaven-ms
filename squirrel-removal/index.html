Our Comprehensive Approach to Squirrel Removal and Exclusion

Initial Inspection and Assessment

Every successful squirrel removal project begins with a thorough inspection. Our skilled technicians will meticulously examine your property to identify key areas of squirrel activity, potential entry points, and the extent of any damage caused. This includes checking attics, crawl spaces, chimneys, vents, and the roofline.

Identifying Entry Points

Squirrels are surprisingly adept at exploiting even small openings. During our inspection, we'll locate all actual and potential entry points, which can include gaps in siding, damaged soffits, uncapped chimneys, and ill-fitting vent covers. Understanding how squirrels are gaining access is crucial for effective removal and exclusion.

Assessing the Extent of Infestation

Determining the number of squirrels present and whether there are young (litters) is vital for planning the removal process. Our assessment helps us choose the most appropriate and humane methods for capture.

Humane Removal Techniques

Our priority is always the safe and humane removal of squirrels. We employ methods that minimize stress to the animals while effectively relocating them away from your property.

Live Trapping and Relocation

In many cases, live trapping is the preferred method. We strategically place traps in areas of high squirrel activity. Once captured, the squirrels are safely transported to a suitable location away from residential areas, allowing them to return to their natural habitat without the possibility of them immediately returning to your property.

Exclusion Devices

Sometimes, a one-way door system is utilized. This allows squirrels to exit the structure but prevents them from re-entering. This is particularly effective when it's confirmed that all squirrels have left the premises.

Damage Repair and Cleanup

Squirrels can leave a mess and cause damage that needs attention. Our service often includes addressing these issues.

Attic and Insulation Remediation

Squirrels frequently nest in attics, compacting and contaminating insulation with urine and feces. Minor structural repairs

We can mend minor damage caused by squirrels chewing on wood, siding, or other building materials, restoring the integrity of your property.

Exclusion and Prevention Strategies

Removing the squirrels is only half the battle. Preventing their return is essential for a long-term solution.

Sealing Entry Points

The most critical step in prevention is sealing all identified entry points. We use durable, squirrel-resistant materials to ensure that squirrels cannot regain access to your home or business. Recognizing the Signs: When to Seek Professional Squirrel Removal in Southaven, MS

Identifying a squirrel problem early can prevent significant damage and expense. Knowing the common signs of squirrel activity on your property in Southaven, MS, is the first step towards addressing the issue. Here are some indicators that you might need professional squirrel removal services:

Hearing Noises in Your Attic or Walls

One of the most common signs of squirrels indoors is unusual sounds coming from your attic, walls, or even ceilings, especially during the daytime. Squirrels are most active during the morning and late afternoon, and you might hear scratching, scurrying, rolling sounds (like nuts), or even chirping or chattering. These noises indicate that squirrels are nesting, moving around, or storing provisions within your structure. While rodents like mice and rats can also cause noise in walls and attics, squirrel sounds are typically louder and occur primarily during daylight hours, whereas rodent control often deals with nocturnal activity. Differentiating between wildlife sounds is part of our expertise.

Squirrels are very active creatures, constantly moving and exploring. The sounds you hear are likely related to them building or expanding their nest, gathering materials, and navigating the confined spaces within your home. The intensity and frequency of the noises can fluctuate depending on the number of squirrels present and their current activities. Don't dismiss these sounds as just 'critters'; they are a clear signal that an unwanted guest has taken up residence. The longer you wait, the more established their presence can become, leading to increased damage and complexity in removal. Timely intervention is key to minimizing the impact.

Discovering Chewed Wires and Insulation

Squirrels possess a natural instinct to gnaw to keep their teeth sharp and growing. When they infiltrate your home, electrical wires and insulation become prime targets for this behavior. Finding chewed wires in your attic, crawl space, or even behind walls is a serious red flag. Damaged wiring poses a significant fire hazard, potentially leading to electrical outages and catastrophic events. Furthermore, squirrels will often tear apart insulation to use as nesting material, reducing your home's energy efficiency and creating drafts. This is a common issue also seen with termite treatment issues, although the causes are vastly different, the need for professional intervention to protect your home's structure and systems remains constant. Both require expert assessment and management.

The damage to wiring and insulation might not be immediately visible unless you access the affected areas. However, unexplained power fluctuations, tripped circuit breakers, or increased energy bills could be secondary indicators of squirrels chewing on electrical components or compromising your home's thermal barrier. Inspecting accessible areas of your attic and crawl space during daylight hours can help you spot these issues early. If you notice any suspicious chewing marks or displaced insulation, it's crucial to contact professionals like Southaven Pest Pros promptly to assess the situation and mitigate the risks. Proactive inspection and action are vital to protecting your property from cumulative damage over time. Small signs often point to larger underlying issues that require expert handling to prevent escalation and ensure safety.

Identifying Entry Points on Your Property

Squirrels are master climbers and can access your home through various openings. Visible signs of entry points are a strong indicator of active infestation or the potential for future problems. Look for chewed or damaged areas around soffits, fascia boards, rooflines, vents (including gable, ridge, and dryer vents), and chimneys – especially uncapped ones. Even small gaps or cracks that may seem insignificant can be exploited by determined squirrels. Sometimes, you might even see grease marks or staining around these entry points from the oils in their fur. These visual clues are direct evidence of squirrels interacting with and attempting to enter or exit your structure. Observing these signs is crucial for early detection and intervention. Identifying these vulnerable spots is a critical part of our initial inspection process. Knowing exactly how they're getting in allows us to develop a precise and effective exclusion plan.

Pay close attention to the condition of your external vents and chimneys during routine property checks. Damaged screens on vents or an uncapped chimney are essentially open invitations for squirrels seeking shelter. Also, examine the points where utility lines enter your home, as squirrels can use these as pathways. Regularly inspecting your roof and siding, especially after storms, can help you spot potential entry points before they become full-blown access routes for wildlife. Addressing these vulnerabilities proactively through repairs and reinforcements is a vital part of preventing squirrel infestations. Our inspection process specifically targets these weak points to ensure comprehensive protection for your property against unwanted wildlife intrusion. Addressing these areas is as important as dealing with active infestations. Prevention is always better than cure when it comes to protecting your home.

Discovering Squirrel Nests or Droppings

Finding nesting materials or evidence of squirrel droppings within your attic, garage, or other sheltered areas of your home is irrefutable proof of their presence. Squirrel nests, often made of leaves, twigs, shredded insulation, and other soft materials, are usually located in warm, secluded spots like attics or wall voids. Squirrel droppings are small, pellet-shaped, and often found in concentrated areas where they frequent. While distinguishing squirrel droppings from those of other pests like rats requires some expertise, their presence in areas like your attic strongly suggest a squirrel problem. Accumulations of droppings can also lead to unpleasant odors and potentially harbor pathogens. The presence of nesting material is a clear indication of an established presence, suggesting they view your home as a long-term shelter. Detecting these signs requires careful observation during inspections of potentially affected areas. Professional identification ensures accurate pest management strategies are employed.

If you venture into your attic or crawl space and notice piles of shredded material, twigs, or an accumulation of small, dark pellets, it's very likely you have squirrels. The odor associated with urine can also be significant. It's important to exercise caution when inspecting these areas, as direct contact with droppings or nesting material without proper protective gear can pose health risks. If you discover these signs, it's best to avoid disturbing the area and contact a professional pest control service. Southaven Pest Pros can safely assess the situation, confirm the presence of squirrels, and initiate the necessary steps for removal and cleanup, including services often associated with silverfish control or cockroach control where infestations require professional cleaning and sanitation. Identifying these signs is crucial for timely intervention to prevent further property damage or health hazards. Our experienced team can differentiate between various pest signs and implement the correct plan.

Witnessing Frequent Squirrel Activity Around Your Home

An increase in squirrel sightings on or around your property, particularly behaviors that seem unusual, can be a strong indicator that they are attempting to gain access or have already found a way in. Observing squirrels repeatedly running along your roofline, climbing walls, or attempting to enter through vents or chimneys goes beyond normal backyard foraging behavior. They may appear more bold or persistent than usual. This heightened activity suggests they are exploring potential entry points or utilizing existing ones to access shelter or nesting sites within your structure. Paying attention to the specific activities and movements of squirrels on your property can provide valuable clues about potential infestation. Increased visibility often correlates with an increased problem. Observing these behaviors helps us understand their routes and habits around your property. For example, if you see them consistently using a specific tree branch to access your roof, that's a key area to focus on for exclusion.

If you see squirrels repeatedly entering and exiting a specific spot on your roof or siding consistently throughout the day, it's a clear sign they have found a way inside. You might also observe them carrying nesting materials, like leaves or twigs, towards your home. While a few squirrels in your yard are normal, persistent and direct interactions with your house structure warrant investigation. Documenting when and where you see this increased activity can be helpful information when you contact a residential or commercial pest professional. Promptly addressing these behavioral indicators can prevent a minor issue from escalating into a major infestation requiring more extensive wildlife control measures. Early detection based on observed behavior can save time and money in the long run by allowing for a targeted and less disruptive intervention strategy. Our team uses behavioral observations to inform our removal and exclusion plans, making them more effective.

Noticing Foul Odors

An unexplained, persistent foul odor emanating from your attic, walls, or other enclosed spaces could be a sign of dead squirrels or accumulated squirrel droppings and urine. When squirrels nest indoors, their waste products can saturate insulation and building materials, producing strong, unpleasant smells. Unfortunately, sometimes squirrels pass away within walls or attics, leading to a decaying odor that can permeate your home. These odors are not only unpleasant but can also attract other pests. The smell associated with squirrel infestations is distinct and often described as musky or pungent, differing from odors caused by bed bug extermination issues or flea treatment needs, which are typically related to bites or visible pest activity, not internal decay or waste accumulation. Recognizing the specific type of odor can help indicate the nature of the pest problem. Our experienced technicians can often identify the source of the odor during an inspection.

If you detect a persistent and worsening foul odor, particularly in upper levels of your home, it is highly advisable to investigate. While the odor itself is problematic, attempting to locate and remove a deceased animal or extensive waste accumulation without proper equipment and knowledge can be challenging and potentially unsanitary. Professionals are equipped to safely locate the source of the odor, remove any deceased animals or contaminated materials, and provide cleaning and sanitation services to eliminate the smell and potential health hazards. Delaying action can allow the odor to become more deeply embedded in building materials and potentially attract secondary infestations of flies or other scavengers. Trusting professionals ensures the source is correctly identified and safely mitigated. Odor is often the distressing sign that prompts homeowners to seek our help, highlighting the urgency of the situation and the need for immediate action to restore air quality and safety.
